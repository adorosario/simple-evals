Sam Pitroda Born on 17 November 1942 in Titlagarh, Odisha, Satyanarayan Gangaram Pitroda, also known as Sam Pitroda, is an Indian telecommunication engineer, entrepreneur, and former chairman of Indian Overseas Congress. He hails from a Gujarati family and has served as an advisor to former Prime Minister Manmohan Singh and the United Nations. Sam Pitroda is widely a telecommunications inventor, entrepreneur, development strategist, and policymaker with half a century of experience in information and communications technology (ICT) and associated global and national advancements. His knowledge got him a role advising Prime Minister Dr Manmohan Singh on ICT in railways and public information infrastructure. About Sam Pitroda Biography Pitroda expanded his contributions by taking on roles such as Chairperson of the National Innovation Council, Chancellor of the Central University of Rajasthan, and Chairperson of Alpha-En Corporation. He established organizations like the India Food Bank and the Global Knowledge Initiative, underlining the significance of social welfare and global cooperation. Moreover, Pitroda’s impact reached international levels, holding positions such as a founding Commissioner of the United Nations Broadband Commission for Digital Development and Chairperson of the International Telecommunication Union’s m-Powering Development Board, promoting the use of mobile technology to empower developing nations.
