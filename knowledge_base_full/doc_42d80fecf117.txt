New Search House History Senate History Copyright Information Privacy Courtesy U.S. Senate Historical Office HART, Thomas Charles (1877-1971) Member XML Senate Years of Service: 1945-1946 Biography HART, THOMAS CHARLES, a Senator from Connecticut; born in Davidson, Genesee County, Mich., June 12, 1877; attended various public schools in Michigan; graduated from the United States Naval Academy, Annapolis, Md., in 1897; served in the Regular Navy from graduation until 1945, when placed on the inactive list as an admiral (retired); his naval career covered service afloat during the Spanish-American War and both World Wars; upon retirement, settled in Sharon, Conn.; appointed on February 15, 1945, as a Republican to the United States Senate to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Francis T. Maloney and served from February 15, 1945, to November 5, 1946; was not a candidate for election to the vacancy in 1946; resided in Sharon, Conn., until his death there on July 4, 1971; interment in Arlington National Cemetery, Arlington, Va. Research Collections Columbia University Oral History Project New York, NY Oral History: 1962. 284 leaves. Reminiscences of childhood and education, naval career, Hart investigation of Pearl Harbor, and career as senator. Microfiche available. Index. U. S. Navy Naval Historical Center Washington, DC Papers: 1897-1967. 2 feet, and 21 volume diary. Chiefly naval papers including correspondence, articles, and diary (1914-1952, 1960), and small collection of personal letters. Finding aid.
